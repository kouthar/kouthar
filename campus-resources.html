YourWaterloo: Engineering in Residence Resource Page

I tackled creating a user-friendly resource page for the first year engineering students who lived in residence at the University of Waterloo. I strived to ensure I designed an experience that was not only user-friendly, but included resources that were immediately relevant in helping my students achieve their academic, personal, and professional goals.

Role: Residence Assistant, Product Designer and UX Researcher/Designer
Time: February 2020 - Present
Skills: User Research, Product Thinking, Wireframing, Prototyping, User Interviewing, Data analytics
Tools: Adobe Illustrator, Adobe XD, Sketch, Invision, Procreate

Background

As a Residence Assistant, I am "live-in" mentor and supervisor for 60+ first year engineering students. I would get information from Residence Life Management to relay to students, and also served as a mentor to learn about my students. I wanted to figure out the best way to support my students to learn and grow as well as keep them updated with relevant information.

Understanding My Users

Problem

1. Sending information & Student engagement

I received several weekly updates from Residence Life Management to relay to students, but if I sent weekly e-mails, the frequency would lead to lower student engagement and my e-mails containing pertinent information would get ignored.

2. Helping my students grow and develop as they want to

I wanted to support my students in developing themselves and feeling comfortable in residence, but I didn't know what was important to them.

To validate what I had in mind, using what I knew from interviews with the users, I further broke down the problem.

User Research

Methods:
1. Interviews
2. Surveys

I conducted unstructured interviews to better understand how my users feel in their current environment. I hypothesised that coop, academics, and social life would be both the root of users worries and goals based on previous knowledge having been a student in their shoes once, too.

By empathising with my users, and asking broad, open-ended questions, without explicitly bringing up my assumptions, I was able to confirm my hypotheses were right, but also why they were feeling certain ways. and what they needed. The survey provided data which supported the insights made from my interviews, validating the problem ensuring I am designing to solve the right challenge.

I created empathy maps, and user flows from the research I conducted, as well as backed up insights with quantitative data. I was able to identify that many of my hypotheses were correct, that my users' goals and worries were related to coop, academics, and community. I also learned my users primarily got their information online, and that they preferred convenience of information so they could quickly judge whether investing more time in an activity is worth their time.

Ideation

I asked myself, "How might I...
...give relevant information to my students?"
...decrease friction in finding opportunities for personal growth?"

From the insights about my users, I identified necessary information that needed to be on the resource page, as well as began outlining information architecture via affinity mapping of information, and weighing pros and cons of different mediums the resource page could be in. Finally, I started wireframing.

Knowing my users' habits, and understanding their needs allowed me to decide to make a website dedicated to their goals and ambitions.

Prototyping & Usability Testing

Before creating my high fidelity mocks and prototypes, I wanted to design a clean guide to faciliate effective visual communication. I thought of a unique, bright branding scheme to highlight positivity and motivation, inspired by vocabulary and resources (ie. the UWaterloo website) that is familiar to my users.

Each colour represented a different section of the website, mirroring the University of Waterloo's style strategy for different faculty pages for continuity and cohesion.

I used a clean, well organised design, with plain language and sans serif font Roboto for accessibility.

Hi-fi Prototypes & Mockups

I established categories with visual cues including whitespace, colour coding, and redundancy. Each page was cohesively designed for continuity of branding to ensure the best experience possible.

In progress! Currently prototyping and iterating on prototypes with user interviews and usability testing!
